Tennessee Cost of Living Overview

Tennessee is significantly more affordable than the national average, with a state-wide cost index of 86. The average monthly rent across Tennessee cities is $1,198, with median household incomes averaging $73,681 per year. The cheapest city is Union (index 49) and the most expensive is Brentwood (index 217).

Tennessee has no state income tax, making it attractive for high earners. However, the 7.0% sales tax and 0.64% property tax rate offset some of those savings.
