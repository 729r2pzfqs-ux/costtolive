Cost of Living Overview

Yakima is significantly cheaper than the national average, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious individuals and families. With a cost index of 80 (where 100 equals the national median), Yakima residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,075/month while earning a median household income of $59,228/year.

The median home value in Yakima, WA is $273,400, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,068 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 21.8% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

Washington has no state income tax, which can represent significant savings compared to high-tax states. Combined with a 6.5% sales tax rate and 0.92% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment helps keep costs manageable in Yakima.
