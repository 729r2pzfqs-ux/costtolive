Cost of Living Overview

Joplin is significantly cheaper than the national average, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious individuals and families. With a cost index of 58 (where 100 equals the national median), Joplin residents can expect to pay median rent of $889/month while earning a median household income of $51,154/year.

The median home value in Joplin, MO is $159,700, with typical monthly owner costs around $886 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 20.9% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

Missouri has a moderate income tax rate of 4.95%. Combined with a 4.225% sales tax rate and 0.93% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment is a factor in the total cost of living in Joplin.
