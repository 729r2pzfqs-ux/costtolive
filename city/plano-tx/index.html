Cost of Living Overview

Plano has a significantly higher cost of living than most US cities, driven largely by premium housing costs. With a cost index of 132 (where 100 equals the national median), Plano residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,792/month while earning a median household income of $108,649/year.

The median home value in Plano, TX is $440,600, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,829 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 19.8% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

Texas has no state income tax, which can represent significant savings compared to high-tax states. Combined with a 6.25% sales tax rate and 1.6% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment helps keep costs manageable in Plano.
