Cost of Living Overview

Montrose offers a below-average cost of living, giving residents more purchasing power compared to many US cities. With a cost index of 90 (where 100 equals the national median), Montrose residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,117/month while earning a median household income of $63,177/year.

The median home value in Montrose, CO is $359,000, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,049 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 21.2% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

Colorado has a moderate income tax rate of 4.4%. Combined with a 2.9% sales tax rate and 0.51% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment is a factor in the total cost of living in Montrose.
