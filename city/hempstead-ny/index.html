Cost of Living Overview

Hempstead has a significantly higher cost of living than most US cities, driven largely by premium housing costs. With a cost index of 131 (where 100 equals the national median), Hempstead residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,649/month while earning a median household income of $82,454/year.

The median home value in Hempstead, NY is $442,100, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,997 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 24.0% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

New York's top income tax rate of 10.9% is among the highest in the nation, adding to the overall cost burden. Combined with a 4.0% sales tax rate and 1.62% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment is a factor in the total cost of living in Hempstead.
