Cost of Living Overview

Puyallup has a significantly higher cost of living than most US cities, driven largely by premium housing costs. With a cost index of 138 (where 100 equals the national median), Puyallup residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,750/month while earning a median household income of $95,639/year.

The median home value in Puyallup, WA is $507,000, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,823 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 22.0% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

Washington has no state income tax, which can represent significant savings compared to high-tax states. Combined with a 6.5% sales tax rate and 0.92% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment helps keep costs manageable in Puyallup.
