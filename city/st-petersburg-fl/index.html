Cost of Living Overview

St. Petersburg is more expensive than the national average. Higher housing costs are typically the primary driver. With a cost index of 106 (where 100 equals the national median), St. Petersburg residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,542/month while earning a median household income of $73,118/year.

The median home value in St. Petersburg, FL is $331,500, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,435 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. With a rent-to-income ratio of 25.3%, St. Petersburg falls within the generally affordable range, though it's approaching the 30% burden threshold.

Florida has no state income tax, which can represent significant savings compared to high-tax states. Combined with a 6.0% sales tax rate and 0.86% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment helps keep costs manageable in St. Petersburg.
