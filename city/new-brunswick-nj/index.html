Cost of Living Overview

New Brunswick is more expensive than the national average. Higher housing costs are typically the primary driver. With a cost index of 117 (where 100 equals the national median), New Brunswick residents can expect to pay median rent of $1,790/month while earning a median household income of $60,248/year.

The median home value in New Brunswick, NJ is $316,800, with typical monthly owner costs around $1,791 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The 35.7% rent-to-income ratio indicates significant housing cost burden. Residents spend well over a third of their income on rent alone.

New Jersey's top income tax rate of 10.75% is among the highest in the nation, adding to the overall cost burden. Combined with a 6.625% sales tax rate and 2.23% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment is a factor in the total cost of living in New Brunswick.
