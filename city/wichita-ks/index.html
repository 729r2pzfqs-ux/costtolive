Cost of Living Overview

Wichita is significantly cheaper than the national average, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious individuals and families. With a cost index of 65 (where 100 equals the national median), Wichita residents can expect to pay median rent of $960/month while earning a median household income of $63,072/year.

The median home value in Wichita, KS is $179,500, with typical monthly owner costs around $994 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 18.3% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

Kansas has a moderate income tax rate of 5.7%. Combined with a 6.5% sales tax rate and 1.41% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment is a factor in the total cost of living in Wichita.
