Cost of Living Overview

Shirley has a significantly higher cost of living than most US cities, driven largely by premium housing costs. With a cost index of 143 (where 100 equals the national median), Shirley residents can expect to pay median rent of $2,110/month while earning a median household income of $105,379/year.

The median home value in Shirley, NY is $390,300, with typical monthly owner costs around $2,262 including mortgage, insurance, and taxes. The median rent-to-income ratio of 24.0% means housing is relatively affordable here — well below the 30% threshold that HUD considers cost-burdened.

New York's top income tax rate of 10.9% is among the highest in the nation, adding to the overall cost burden. Combined with a 4.0% sales tax rate and 1.62% effective property tax rate, the overall tax environment is a factor in the total cost of living in Shirley.
