📊 Data Sources

All data comes from official US government sources:

  • U.S. Census Bureau — ACS 2023
    Median rent, income, home values, owner costs, population
  • Tax Foundation — 2024
    State income tax, sales tax, and property tax rates

🧮 Cost Index Methodology

Our cost index uses the national median as a baseline of 100. A city with an index of 120 is 20% more expensive than average; an index of 80 is 20% cheaper.

The index is a weighted composite:
40% Median Rent + 35% Home Value + 25% Owner Costs

📏 Affordability Ratio

The rent-to-income ratio shows what percentage of median household income goes to rent. HUD considers below 30% as affordable. Above 30% is cost-burdened.

⚠️ Disclaimer

This tool is for informational purposes only. Actual costs vary by neighborhood, lifestyle, and personal circumstances. Data reflects city-wide medians and may not represent specific areas within a city.
